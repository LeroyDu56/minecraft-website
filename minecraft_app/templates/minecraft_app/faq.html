Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions? We Have Answers

Find answers to the most common questions about our server

What is GeoMC?

GeoMC is a Minecraft server running on a 1:1000 scale map of Earth. Players can create towns, join nations, and engage in diplomacy, trade, or warfare, all on a realistic representation of our world.

How do I join the server?

To join GeoMC, you need Minecraft Java Edition version 1.20.4. Open Minecraft, go to Multiplayer, click "Add Server", and enter play.geomc.fr as the server address. Click "Done" and then connect to the server!

Is the server free to play?

Yes, GeoMC is completely free to play! We offer optional donation ranks in our store that provide various perks, but they're not required to enjoy the full Towny experience.

Do you have a Discord server?

Yes! We have an active Discord community where you can chat with other players, get help from staff, and stay updated on server events. You can join through the Discord button in the website footer or by using this link: https://discord.gg/a8G7wUKp2Y

What plugins does the server use?

Our server uses several plugins to enhance gameplay, including Towny, EssentialsX, GriefPrevention, mcMMO, Dynmap, and several custom plugins tailored specifically for our Earth map experience.

Is PvP enabled on the server?

PvP is disabled in peaceful towns and neutral areas but can be enabled in designated PvP zones or between towns/nations that have declared war on each other. This creates a balanced environment where players can choose their level of engagement in conflicts.

Are there any custom features?

Yes! We have several custom features including realistic resource distribution based on real-world geography, unique nation abilities reflective of their real-world counterparts, custom events, and more. Our goal is to provide a unique Earth-based gameplay experience.

How do I claim land?

Land claiming is done through the Towny system. First, you need to create or join a town using /town new [town name] or /town join [town name]. Once in a town, mayors or assistants can claim land using /town claim while standing in the chunk you want to claim. Each claim costs money from the town bank.

How do I create a town?

To create a town, use the command /town new [town name]. You need $2,000 to create a town. Choose your location carefully, as your first claim will be your town spawn. Remember that towns should be created in locations that make geographical sense on our Earth map.

How do I create or join a nation?

To create a nation, you must first be a mayor of a town. Then use /nation new [nation name]. Creating a nation costs $25,000 from your town bank. To join an existing nation, the mayor of your town needs to accept an invitation from a nation's leader using /nation join [nation name] after receiving an invite.

What are the benefits of joining a nation?

Nations provide various benefits including:

  • Protection through alliances and mutual defense
  • Nation-specific bonuses based on real-world characteristics
  • Access to nation-owned resources and lands
  • Reduced teleport costs between towns in the same nation
  • Nation chat channel for communication
  • Participation in nation-level events and warfare

Are there any rules about town/nation names?

Yes, town and nation names should be appropriate and follow our naming guidelines:

  • Names should fit the Earth theme (preferably real-world locations)
  • No offensive, inappropriate, or explicit names
  • No names that mimic server ranks or staff positions
  • No excessive special characters or formatting
  • Nation names should generally be based on real countries or historical nations

Staff reserves the right to rename towns/nations that violate these guidelines.

How does the economy work?

GeoMC has a player-driven economy with a server currency. You can earn money through various activities like trading, completing jobs, selling items to shops, and participating in events. Money is used for claiming land, paying town taxes, purchasing items, and more.

How do I earn money?

There are several ways to earn money on GeoMC:

  • Selling resources to server or player shops
  • Completing jobs or tasks using the Jobs plugin
  • Trading with other players
  • Participating in server events and competitions
  • Creating and running businesses or services
  • Mining and resource gathering (some areas have more valuable resources based on real geography)

What are the town upkeep costs?

Towns have daily upkeep costs that scale with their size. The base cost is $10 per day, plus $5 per claimed chunk. Nations have an upkeep cost of $100 per day plus $15 per member town. Make sure to keep enough money in your town/nation bank to cover these costs, or your town may fall into ruin!

How do player shops work?

Players can create their own shops using chest shops. To create a shop:

  1. Place a chest and add the items you want to sell
  2. Place a sign on the chest
  3. Format the sign with [quantity] [B/S/BS] [price] [item name optional]
  4. B = Buy, S = Sell, BS = Both

For example: 64 S $100 would sell 64 items for $100.

What Minecraft version should I use?

Our server currently runs on Minecraft version 1.20.4. For the best experience, you should use the same version. We update the server to newer versions after extensive testing to ensure stability.

Are mods allowed?

We allow client-side mods that don't provide unfair advantages. Allowed mods include:

  • OptiFine and other performance enhancers
  • Minimaps (without cave or entity radar features)
  • Cosmetic mods (shaders, resource packs)
  • Interface enhancements (inventory sorters, damage indicators)

Prohibited mods include X-ray, fly hacks, speed hacks, auto-clickers, and any mod that provides an unfair gameplay advantage.

I'm experiencing lag, what can I do?

If you're experiencing lag, try these steps:

  1. Check your own internet connection and restart your router if needed
  2. Allocate more RAM to Minecraft in the launcher settings
  3. Install performance-enhancing mods like OptiFine or Sodium
  4. Reduce your render distance and graphics settings
  5. Close other applications running in the background

If server-wide lag is occurring, our staff team is likely already working to resolve it. You can check our Discord for server status updates.

How do I link my Discord account?

To link your Discord account with your Minecraft account:

  1. Join our Discord server
  2. Go to the #bot-commands channel
  3. Type !link
  4. The bot will give you a unique code
  5. Log into our Minecraft server and type /discord link [your code]
  6. Your accounts are now linked, and you'll receive any applicable Discord roles based on your in-game rank
